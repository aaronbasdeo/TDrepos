Glossary

Jump to terms that start with... [ A | C | D | F | G | I | M | N | O | P | Q | R | S | T | U | V ]

A

active

Depending on context, one of the following.

active discounts — Configured discounts, during a time while they are valid and can be redeemed. See discounts.

active orders — Orders for which all issued invoices (if any) are paid and which, in the case of time-limited subscriptions, are not stopped due to suspension, cancellation, or expiration. See order.

active user — Although a registered user who has not yet activated his or her account on your marketplace cannot enter the purchase flow to obtain any product, it is possible that he or she could consume one of the seat assignments that a product contract allocates. When this happens, it happens via our invite and assign feature.

ADDON_CANCEL event

The integration API event type that fires when the active order for a given addon is canceled, whether manually by the consumer or automatically by the billing system. See integration APIs.

addon_change event

The integration API event type that fires when a consumer changes his or her active order for an addon. For example, the consumer might double how many units (such as hours, gigabytes, messages, records, or what have you) he or she is entitled to consume through the addon. See integration APIs.

addon_order event

The integration event that tells a vendor’s API endpoint when a product’s authorized user has ordered an addon to supplement the corresponding product. See integration APIs.

addon_unbind event

The integration API event type that fires when a given addon is divorced from whichever product it was formerly able to supplement. After the addon is unbound from its parent product, the addon is no longer available for consumers to order on your marketplace. See integration APIs.

adjustment

Channel administrators can review any scheduled invoice before the billed party receives it, and edit the balance due. See invoices.

AppDirect share

The revenue sharing percentage that AppDirect receives from any income that a given product generates.

assisted sale

A product order placed on a consumer’s behalf by authorized marketplace support staff or authorized outside resellers. See order and sales agents.

asynchronous pending events

Some vendor endpoints respond more slowly than others do to product integration API events. For example, the endpoints for a product that necessitates manual provisioning do not respond immediately when your marketplace transmits events to them. Instead, their delayed (asynchronous) response to an API event is considered pending until it occurs. See integration APIs.

C

canceled

Depending on context, one of the following.

An order whose owner canceled it in either of two ways.

manual — The subscription owner cancels his or her previously active subscription explicitly. Subscription contract stipulations determine when the cancellation takes effect in real life, whether any termination fees apply, and when if ever the subscription owner might be able to obtain a new contract for this product.

automatic — The subscription owner opts to let a previously active subscription lapse without renewal, which was renewable until a grace period elapsed.

A subscription whose owner canceled it.

carried invoice

An invoice whose balance due is deferred to a subsequent invoice. See invoices.

CVV

card verification value — A short numeric string — usually three or four characters long — that is not visible on the front of a billable party’s credit card and is not impressible. [Do credit card companies use CVV numbers outside of North America? I know that credit card technologies differ overseas.]

D

discounts

Methods by which a product vendor or a marketplace provider can reduce consumer expense while promoting one product in particular or encouraging conversions generally. At a high level, there are two discount types.

fixed price discount — A coupon (voucher) that reduces consumer costs by a set amount of whichever national or regional currency your marketplace uses as its default. For example, a marketplace whose default currency is USD might offer a fixed-priced discount of $20.

percentage discount — A coupon (voucher) that reduces consumer costs by a whole number percentage of their charged amount. For example, your marketplace might offer a discount of 30 percent.

discount code

A string of characters that identifies one configured discount generally for administrative purposes.

When buyers cannot apply a discount to an order without entering a text string manually, the correct text string is exactly this same code.

F

failed

Depending on context, one of the following.

failed orders — Order creation fails when the vendor is not able to provision consumer access to the product. Contact the vendor to learn what happened. See order.

failed payments — When a payment fails, the cause is usually a payment gateway timeout. See payment.

failed payouts — Payouts can fail for any number of reasons. For example, your marketplace might have stored an invalid email address or invalid PayPal account for the vendor, or might have insufficient funds to cover the transaction. Alternatively, there could be a payment gateway timeout. See payout.

free trial

Depending on context, one of the following.

A product edition that is not billable, which might support limited use over a wide time frame or wide use over a limited time frame. Upon their expiration date, if any, some free-trial editions roll over automatically to a recurring and billable edition of the same product.

A purchase order whose current state is FREE TRIAL. This state can transition to any of these states: ACTIVE, ONE TIME, FREE TRIAL EXPIRED, or FREE TRIAL CANCELED. See edition.

free trial expired

Depending on context, one of the following.

A consumer’s expired contract to use a product’s time-limited free trial edition.

A purchase order whose current state is FREE TRIAL EXPIRED. This state can be the terminal state of a purchase order or can transition to FREE TRIAL CANCELED.

See edition.

frequency

How often (monthly, for example) invoicing recurs against a billable subscription contract. See invoices.

G

gross revenue

Depending on context, one of the following.

At a high level, all marketplace-related revenue, without accounting for any direct or indirect costs that AppDirect, the marketplace provider, all product vendors collectively, and all resellers may incur collectively through their participation in the marketplace.

At a low level, all revenue related to one product in particular, without accounting for any direct or indirect costs that AppDirect, the marketplace provider, the product’s vendor, and any involved resellers may incur individually or collectively through their sales, provisioning, and ongoing support of that one product.

I

inactive

Depending on context, one of the following.

inactive company — A company whose first user, usually the company administrator, has not yet activated his or her user account on your marketplace.

inactive discount — A configured discount that is not currently available to be redeemed. See discounts.

inactive user — A registered user who has not yet activated his or her user account.

initialized order

A newly placed order starts its life in the INITIALIZED state when the following three statements are true simultaneously.

The order specifies a hosted product whose vendor must provision it.

The order should take effect immediately, and therefore is not post-dated.

The order does not apply any discount.

Alternatively, the purchase order for a billable and auto-renewable product edition can begin in the INITIALIZED state, upon contract term renewal, when either of the following statements is true.

You or another channel administrator blocked in-term contract changes for all products, sitewide.

The vendor blocked in-term contract changes for this product in particular.

See order.

Or, to understand purchase order state transitions, see Appendix A, [%=UG_2015-Q3revA.Chapter Title Name%].

integration APIs

The AppDirect-developed set of API endpoints that your marketplace exposes to outside developers — supposing that your organization (the marketplace provider) supports direct product integrations from third-party products and services. Vendors use integration APIs to communicate bidirectionally with your marketplace about consumers that you share in common with them, regarding all transactions where your mutual consumers order a product edition, allocate its seats, configure it, consume its units or licensed capacity, pay its invoices, and so on.

invite and assign

A workflow that some marketplaces are configured to support, where company administrators can assign product seats even to invited, prospective users who have not yet activated their marketplace user accounts.

invoices

Consumer-facing and billing period-specific billing statements for a cited order, which specify the due date, the amount due, and any past-due amount.

Where the consumer’s product contract spreads his or her payment obligation across multiple billing cycles, each invoice represents only a fraction of the consumer’s full obligation. Alternatively, when the consumer’s product contract demands a lump-sum payment in full, the corresponding invoice shows his or her entire obligation for payment.

invoice ID

A numeric string that identifies one invoice uniquely per marketplace.

M

manual

Depending on context, one of the following.

manual payment — A notation that you or a sales support representative can make to record when consumers satisfy one of their payment obligations outside of your marketplace, such as by check (cheque).

manual payout — A notation that you can make to record when a vendor payout took place outside of your marketplace, such as by check (cheque).

manual refund — You can refund any consumer payment in its entirety — using the Refund Payment button at Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Billing | Payments > {payment} — or choose only a subset of the payment’s associated invoices whose paid amount should be returned.

manual resolution — An obligation, which you might face in the Pending Events table — at Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Events | Event Logs > (Pending Events) — to work directly with a product’s vendor on solving a consumer access provisioning problem cooperatively.

marketplace share

The revenue sharing percentage that the marketplace provider receives from any income that a given product generates.

N

net revenue

Depending on context, one of the following.

At a high level, all marketplace-related revenue, after accounting for any direct or indirect costs that AppDirect, the marketplace provider, all product vendors collectively, and all resellers incurred collectively through their participation in the marketplace.

At a low level, all revenue related to one product in particular, after accounting for all direct and indirect costs that AppDirect, the marketplace provider, the product’s vendor, and all involved resellers incurred individually and collectively through their sales, provisioning, and ongoing support of that one product.

O

one‑time

A billable product whose billing never recurs after the initial payment, which is payment in full.

order ID

A unique numeric identifier for one order on one marketplace.

order status

An order transitions through multiple states from the moment of its creation to the moment of its conclusion. Each state has a name that distinguishes it from other order states. For example, an order might transition from PENDING REMOTE CREATION to ACTIVE to SUSPENDED to ACTIVE to FINISHED.

order

See order.

P

payment

A financial transaction in which a consumer directs funds toward resolving a debt and abiding by a contractual obligation. The currency, payment methods, taxation, and so on, for a given payment are always ones that your marketplace is configured to support and apply.

payment ID

A unique numeric identifier for one received payment on one marketplace.

payment instrument

The combination of one billable party’s payment method and all corresponding details about his or her account with the payment method provider. Details include:

billing information (name, address, telephone number, and so on)

company association

account expiration date

CVV [Is this for North America exclusively?]

payment method

Variable by geography and by the provider’s implemented preference, each mechanism by which it is possible for a billed party to tender payment — such as credit card, debit card, check (cheque), PayPal, or the provider’s existing systems for centralized billing.

payout

See payout.

payout ID

A unique numeric identifier for one payout on one marketplace.

payout ownership

The party that is responsible for issuing a given payout is said to be the payout owner.

payout status

A vendor payout can exist in any of four states.

FAILED — A payout that failed for any number of possible reasons. For example, your marketplace might have stored an invalid email address or invalid PayPal account for the vendor, or have insufficient funds to cover the transaction. Alternatively, there could be a payment gateway timeout. See payout.

MANUAL — A notation that you can make to record when a vendor payout took place outside of your marketplace, such as by check (cheque).

PENDING — A payout that is initiated but has not been processed. PENDING is a transient state and no payout should be committed with it.

SUCCESSFUL — A payout that was processed successfully.

pending

Depending on context, one of the following.

pending orders — One of these:

PENDING MIGRATION ACTIVATION — An order whose associated user account was migrated from an AppDirect competitor’s platform. Provisioning of product access cannot begin until the relevant user activates his or her user account, post-migration.

PENDING REMOTE CREATION — An order enters this state when your marketplace notifies the service vendor that an order was placed, which requires provisioning. PENDING REMOTE CREATION has, conditionally, two sub‑states:

PENDING ASYNCHRONOUS CREATION — Most vendors provision access automatically, in something like real time, upon notification by your marketplace that access should be provisioned. Ordinarily, when provisioning does not occur promptly, the corresponding order transitions from PENDING REMOTE CREATION to FAILED. However, when a product’s vendor has specified (during integration) that it provisions access manually instead of automatically, relevant orders transition from PENDING REMOTE CREATION to PENDING ASYNCHRONOUS CREATION, a transient state that does not have any hard-coded timeout.

PENDING MANUAL RECOVERY — When your marketplace has manual event recovery enabled and anyone orders a product whose provisioning should occur automatically (due to settings that its vendor chose during integration), any order in the PENDING REMOTE CREATION state that becomes stalled transitions to PENDING MANUAL RECOVERY rather than FAILED — as it would on a marketplace where manual event recovery is not enabled.

PENDING USER APPROVAL — An order created as part of an assisted sale, whose target user has not yet consented explicitly to the order as it was eventually submitted,

pending payouts — A payout is PENDING when it is initiated but has not been processed. PENDING is a transient state and no payout should be committed with it.

Q

quote

An optional and preliminary record of, and a way to return to, the interrupted purchase flow from an assisted sale that is not yet complete. Sales agents can generate a quote during the course of any assisted sale. Then, any channel administrator or reseller administrator can complete the purchase flow, edit the quote, or delete it.

R

reconciliation

The process by which any need for payouts is calculated.

refund

The return of funds to a consumer, which he or she paid toward an invoice.

reseller

See reseller.

revenue shares

The contractually agreed-upon revenue sharing percentages that AppDirect, a marketplace provider, and a product vendor all receive respectively from their mutually beneficial sales of a given product.

S

sales agents

The set of registered users, including resellers and sales support representatives, who are authorized and empowered to buy, update, upgrade, and downgrade product subscriptions on behalf of another user. See assisted sale.

sales lead

Basic contact information for a potential customer, whom no one associated with your marketplace has qualified.

sales opportunity

A potential customer who has been qualified as a good match for your marketplace and who is ready to start doing business with you.

subscription_cancel event

The product integration API event type that fires when a subscription is canceled. See integration APIs.

subscription_change event

The product integration API event type that fires when a subscription is switched from one edition to another, from one number of seats to another, or from one allotment of consumable units to another. See integration APIs.

subscription_create event

The product integration API event type that fires when a vendor must provision access. See integration APIs.

subscription_notice event

The product integration API event type that fires when a given subscription’s provisioning state changes — thus requiring the vendor to take swift and specific action. The provisioning-related SUBSCRIPTION_NOTICE types are as follows:

CLOSED — The vendor should delete the subscriber’s account. The order that underlies the described subscription has been in either the SUSPENDED state or the FREE_TRIAL_EXPIRED state for a period of time that exceeds the marketplace-configurable grace period, which is typically 1 or 2 months. In most cases, the vendor’s response to a SUBSCRIPTION_NOTICE event of type CLOSED is identical to the vendor’s response to a SUBSCRIPTION_CANCEL event.

DEACTIVATED — The vendor should suspend the subscriber’s access to the product but not delete the subscriber’s account. Payment is past due or abuse has been detected.

REACTIVATED — The vendor should restore access that was previously suspended. The subscriber’s infraction has been resolved.

UPCOMING_INVOICE — Because marketplace computation of the subscriber’s outstanding bill is scheduled to begin in exactly 24 hours, the vendor should submit a Billing Usage API call that communicates the subscriber’s usage data.

subscription

One possible result of an order is that the consumer assumes the obligation to pay an ordered product’s recurring cost at predefined intervals (the billing cycle) until the contract’s defined end date. This consumer obligation is paired with the vendor’s obligation to provide exactly the described service under exactly the defined service level agreement. Jointly, the consumer obligation and the vendor obligation constitute a subscription. Compare to one‑time.

successful

Because a payout was processed without any significant errors or delays, its specified payee received the specified funds on or before its specified due date, and these funds were attributed correctly to the specified payer.

suspended

Depending on context, one of the following.

In most cases where an order becomes SUSPENDED, it happens automatically because a payment is past due, even beyond any configured grace period.

Rarely and as a separate matter, you or another channel administrator might mark an order SUSPENDED manually due to service disruptions, contract violations, or other unusual circumstances.

T

termination fee

A penalty that some product subscription contracts stipulate, which must be paid by the consumer who cancels his or her subscription before a certain date.

top customers

You or any other channel administrator can populate a list of marquee customers — at Manage > Channel > Products > Product Content | Top Customers — whose corporate logos are then available for citation, in the form of badges, by vendors on their products’ marketing profile pages.

total orders

The count of all orders created, relative to the context where cited. For example, the ‘total orders’ value associated with one company is usually a different count than the ‘total orders’ value for your marketplace as a whole.

U

user_assignment event

The product integration API event type that fires when the vendor must allocate one of an ordered product’s still-available seats to a particular consumer.

user_unassignment event

The product integration API event type that fires when the vendor must withdraw one of an ordered product’s consumed seats from the particular consumer who consumed it.

user_updated event

The product integration API event type that fires when an already-provisioned product account must be modified to provide a changed quantity of units.

V

vendor share

The revenue sharing percentage that a product vendor receives from any income that a given product generates.
