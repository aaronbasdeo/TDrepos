The sequence of transitions in Scenario 34

The chronology of transitions in this scenario is as follows.

Order and subscription state transitions in Scenario 34

 

A purchase order starts its life in the UPCOMING state when both of the following statements — about a time-limited but renewable product entitlement — are true simultaneously:

The order specifies a product edition that its end-user already has, via a previous purchase order, which is still currently ACTIVE. Therefore, no provisioning is required. (Typically, the previous order applied a discount which is valid toward fewer billing cycles than the full contract term should span. For example, suppose that the discount was valid during one or two billing cycles, but the contract term spans twelve billing cycles. In this case, that ACTIVE order, with its discount applied, is paired with this UPCOMING order, which does not apply any discount.)

The order is post-dated, taking effect sometime in the future.

The subscription state is ACTIVE.

 

This order’s transition to the ACTIVE state occurs at exactly the same moment when its predecessor order transitions to the FINISHED state.

The subscription state is ACTIVE.

 

Transition to a CANCELED state occurs (manually in this case) when the consumer requests to terminate the subscription that a given order covers (rather than merely updating the subscribed edition or units). However, stipulations of the corresponding subscription contract might:

Mandate that the subscription must continue, uninterrupted, through a minimum stipulated number of billing cycles.

Impose penalties for early cancellation.

Therefore, cancellation itself might take effect after a future billing cycle concludes.

The subscription state is CANCELED.
