Orders (Editions) page element reference

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Billing | Orders > [Editions]

UI fields and elements on the Editions page

Element

Description

Products/Editions (toggle)

Toggles the representation of orders to center on products generally or on product editions specifically.

Show Filters (button)

Exposes a banner above the table, where UI elements can help you to shape which records the table should represent — both in its visible rows and, optionally, in any CSV file that you generate and download.

 

Status (list) — One of the following. Or Show All, the full combination of all the following.

 

PENDING_REMOTE_CREATION — A purchase order endures in the PENDING REMOTE CREATION state during any interval between which (A.) your marketplace notifies the product vendor that an order was placed, which requires provisioning, and (B.) the vendor provisions it.

 

FREE_TRIAL — A purchase order transitions to the FREE TRIAL state when its vendor finishes provisioning the consumer’s access to its specified free trial edition.

 

ACTIVE — The purchase order for a recurrently billable product transitions to the ACTIVE state when its vendor finishes provisioning the consumer’s access to it, and persists in this state until any subsequent transition to a SUSPENDED, CANCELED, or FINISHED state.

 

FINISHED — A purchase order can transition to the FINISHED state only when it becomes obsolete. This happens as a replacement purchase order takes effect, which continues or extends essentially the same contract. For example, the original purchase order, now FINISHED, might apply to year 1 of a subscription that, upon its renewal, spawned a new and ACTIVE purchase order to cover year 2.

 

ONE_TIME — The purchase order for a product’s billable but non-recurring edition transitions to the ONE-TIME state when the vendor finishes provisioning the consumer’s access to it. And sometimes, this transition occurs in a context where the consumer has updated his or her contract from one of the product’s free-trial editions to its billable but non-recurring edition. Alternatively, the transition to a ONE-TIME state occurs when its vendor finishes provisioning a billable product whose billing does not recur.

 

CANCELED — A purchase order transitions to the CANCELED state manually when the consumer cancels the associated subscription OR automatically when a contract that does not renew automatically nonetheless stipulates a grace period during which the consumer is permitted to renew manually — and then the consumer does not renew it.

 

SUSPENDED — A purchase order transitions to the SUSPENDED state when one of its associated invoices is not paid — and, in the case of a product edition that allows a grace period for late payments, remains unpaid all through its grace period.

 

FREE_TRIAL_EXPIRED — A purchase order transitions to the FREE TRIAL EXPIRED state when the associated product’s free trial period has elapsed without its consumer ever upgrading to one of the same product’s billable editions. However, this occurs only in the absence of a defined grace period, which otherwise triggers the FREE TRIAL CANCELED state.

 

FREE_TRIAL_CANCELED — A purchase order transitions to the FREE TRIAL CANCELED state manually when its consumer requests to terminate the associated subscription to a time-limited, no-cost edition. Cancellation of a free trial version takes effect immediately.

 

FAILED — A purchase order transitions to the FAILED state when the vendor is not able to provision the specified product.

 TIP:  We recommend that you investigate the basis for this failure and work with the vendor to resolve it — both to benefit the affected consumer right now and so that no similar failures affect your other consumers in the future.

 

UPCOMING — The purchase order for a renewable product subscription starts its life in the UPCOMING state when the order specifies a product edition that its consumer already has, via a previous purchase order, which is still currently ACTIVE. Therefore, no vendor provisioning is required AND the order is post-dated, taking effect sometime in the future. (Typically, the previous order applied a discount which is valid toward fewer billing cycles than the full contract term should span. For example, suppose that the discount was valid during one or two billing cycles, but the contract term spans twelve billing cycles. In this case, that ACTIVE order, with its discount applied, is paired with this UPCOMING order, which does not apply any discount.)

 

Cost (list) — One of the following.

 

Show All — The full combination of all ordered products.

Paid — Billable products exclusively.

Free — Non-billable products exclusively.

 

Created between (date picker) — Define the start and end of a date range by entering dates manually in the paired date-entry fields, using the notation syntax that is standard in your locale and which your marketplace is configured to use — either MDYY or DMYY. Alternatively, click a field’s corresponding calendar icon () to expose the date-picker widget for populating that field.

Download CSV (button)

Generate and download to your client system a comma-separated values (CSV) file, whose records are the underlying basis for the Editions table as you filtered or otherwise shaped it.

ID (column)

Cites a unique, numeric identifier, often seven digits long, that designates the described purchase order.

Created (column)

Cites the date when the described order was created, using notation syntax that is standard in your locale and which your marketplace is configured to use — either MDYY or DMYY.

Status (column)

Specifies the current state of the described order. See [%=UG_2015-Q3revA.Chapter Title Name%].

Company (column)

Identifies the company whose product contract is tied to the described invoice.

Product (column)

Identifies the contracted product by name.

Edition (column)

Identifies the contracted product edition by name.

Frequency (column)

Cites the billing frequency, if any, that applies to the described purchase order’s associated product contract.

Total Fee (column)

Shows a running total and forecast of the payable amount that is scheduled to come due on the next upcoming invoice.

(pagination controls)

Click these UI elements under the table to jump between respectively distant ranges of records in it, when it contains more records than it is configured to render at one time as visible rows.
