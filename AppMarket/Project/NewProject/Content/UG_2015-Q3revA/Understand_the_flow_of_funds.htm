Understand the flow of funds

An AppDirect-powered marketplace is multisided. It involves multiple parties that all bear financial obligations of one kind or another toward one another. Funds must flow from one party to another seamlessly in certain patterns at certain times. The patterns are as follows.

Phase One: transaction

A consumer buys a billable product through a marketplace.

AppDirect meters and rates the billing.

The marketplace provider charges the consumer’s payment instrument, collects funds, and recognizes top-line revenue.

>

Phase Two: reconciliation

AppDirect reconciles each purchase in real time, based on the contractual revenue sharing agreement among the marketplace provider, the product vendor, and AppDirect. Optionally, a reseller could also participate in some cases.

AppDirect invoices the marketplace provider for any portion of collected funds that are rightfully payable to the product vendor, AppDirect itself, or any participating reseller.

>

Phase Three: payout

The marketplace provider pays AppDirect the invoiced amount.

AppDirect disperses their contractually committed percentages of this money to the product vendor and reseller, and keeps the remainder — its rightful share.
