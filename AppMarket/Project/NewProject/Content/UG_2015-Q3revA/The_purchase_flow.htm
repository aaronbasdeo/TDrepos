The purchase flow

When you click the Buy Now or Try Now button on a product profile, you enter a linear sequence of UI pages that we call the purchase flow for that described product. A purchase flow guides you sequentially through the end-to-end process to buy or try the described product. A typical purchase flow includes four pages. However, some products carry additional prerequisites, which inject a supplemental form that the buyer must also complete when prompted.  

Pages in the purchase flow 

Create Order

The Create Order page guides you through your choice of a product edition, invites you to enter a discount code (voucher) if you have one, and shows a running total for your purchase, which might not account fully for every tax and fee that your marketplace is obliged by its government to collect.

 

When you are ready to proceed, click Continue.

Product Settings

(Not standard)

In some cases, the purchase flow includes a Product Settings page. We show this page in the purchase flow only when provisioning of your product account is dependent upon one or more nonstandard prerequisites.

For example, the product vendor — Microsoft or Google, let’s say — could require that your company demonstrate its ownership of an Internet domain to be associated with your subscription.

 

When you are ready to proceed, click Continue.

Billing Details

The Billing Details page guides you through your choice of a payment method generally (limited only by which methods your preferred marketplace is configured to support), and then collects details that demonstrate your control over a specific account.

 

When you are ready to proceed, click Continue.

Alternatively, if you see that anything about your order is wrong, click Back so you can start to correct any error or oversight.

Confirm Order

The Confirm Order page invites you to check that all of your purchase selections thus far were as you intended them to be.

 

When you are ready to proceed, check the checkbox that confirms your agreement to various policies, and then click Place Order.

Alternatively, if you see that anything about your order is wrong, click Back so you can start to correct any error or oversight.

Order Receipt

The Order Receipt page is a record for you to save, which cites the details of what you ordered, how and when you ordered it, and possibly other details.
