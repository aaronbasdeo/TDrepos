From the Create New Product page, name your product, and choose a service type, integration type, usage model, and revenue model for it. 

Complete the following procedure to create a new product from the Developer Center.

Procedure

Step 1. Go to Manage > Billing and Distribution > Products. The Manage Your Products page appears.

Step 2. Click Add Products. The Create New Product page appears. All fields in the image are described after it.

Tip: The default product type  is Web Application, and it is not configurable on the Create New Product page. However, if that product type does not meet your needs, contact your AppDirect technical representative. 

Step 3. Enter the product name. This is the name that marketplace customers and Company Administrators see when they shop for and purchase your product, and when they use and manage the product.

Step 4. Select one of the following service type options:

  • Standalone Product—Does not need to be associated with another product before it can be listed, sold, and used on a marketplace.
  • Add-on for Product—Requires an association with a standalone product before it can be listed, sold, and used on a marketplace.

Step 5. Select one of the integration type options. This determines if the product is automatically provisioned, or not.

  • Referral Only—Products have a profile on the marketplace for marketing purposes only. Leads generated by the product's profile are redirected to the developer's website. Sales for referral products are not completed on the marketplace.
  • Full Integration—Requires that you completely integrate the product with AppDirect. The marketplace handles purchases, service provisioning, and user management. The marketplace also tracks all data, so that you can track sales.

Step 6. Select one of the usage model options. The usage model determines the way in which users access the application. It is important to select the right usage model, since it cannot be changed after the product is created.

  • Single User—Allows only one user account to access, and use the product. Typically, user access to the product is managed by Company Administrators. 
  • Multiple Users—Allows multiple user accounts to access, and use the product. Typically, Company Administrators assign products to users. 

Step 7. Select one of the Revenue Model options to determine how you charge for your product.

  • Free—Product requires no payment. It is free of charge.
  • One time—Product requires one payment in full.
  • Recurring—Product requires multiple payments on a developer-defined schedule, for example, monthly.
  • Tiered—Product includes various unit increments for sale at a price per unit. This pricing model allows developers to offer price breaks to customers as they purchase more units.

Step 8. Click Create Product. The product is now created, and appears on the Product Dashboard.

From the Product Dashboard, you can edit the product profile and branding, add and manage editions of the product, edit settings and discounts, as required. These functionalities are described starting in the next section.

Note:  Some elements are configured by default when you click Create Product. For example, you cannot set the product's price for the product during the initial product creation, but a default price is set by the system. You can edit the product after it has been created  to ensure it is fully configured to meet your needs.  This guide provides more information about these topics, including: Edit profiles and branding, Edit editions and pricing, Edit integration settings, Edit device settings, and Edit settings.
