The sequence of transitions in Scenario 31

The chronology of transitions in this scenario is as follows.

Order and subscription state transitions in Scenario 31

 

A newly placed order starts its life in the PENDING USER APPROVAL state when a reseller (or other authorized representative of your marketplace) has placed an order on behalf of a registered user.

There is no subscription state at this stage. A subscription does not yet exist.

 

Transition to a PENDING REMOTE CREATION state occurs after the registered user consents to this assisted purchase — and after your marketplace notifies the product vendor that an order was placed, which requires provisioning (and here, the requested edition is a free trial version) — when all of these statements are true simultaneously:

The order specifies a hosted product whose vendor must provision it.

The order should take effect immediately, and therefore is not post-dated.

The order does not apply any discount.

The subscription state is INITIALIZED.

 

Transition to the FREE TRIAL state occurs when the relevant vendor finishes provisioning. The consumer is now truly subscribed to the free trial version of an otherwise billable product.

The subscription state is FREE TRIAL.

 

Finally, the transition to a FREE TRIAL EXPIRED state occurs when the free trial period has elapsed — in the absence of any defined grace period, which might otherwise have triggered the FREE TRIAL CANCELED state — without its consumer ever upgrading to one of the same product’s billable editions.

The subscription state is FREE TRIAL EXPIRED.
