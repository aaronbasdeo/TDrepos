Work with the Product Purchase Event template

You can edit content in the Product Purchase Event template and turn its output On or Off. This template is optional. You can stop it from sending email notification messages.

When the template is turned On, it sends an informational message to the developer whose product was purchased.

When the template is turned Off, the developer is not notified that his or her product was purchased.

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Settings > Custom UI | Customer Notifications > Custom Product Emails | Product Purchase Event

Before you begin

Log in to your marketplace as its administrator.

Make sure that your marketplace has a Developer Center.

Procedure

Go to Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Settings > Custom UI | Customer Notifications.

Click Product Purchase Event in the Custom Product Emails area.

(Optional) Slide the Enabled toggle right to turn the template On.

OR

Slide the Enabled toggle left to turn the template Off.

Enter an appropriate email subject line in the Subject field. For example, your subject line might be: A {PARTNER_STORE_LABEL} user just ordered {APPLICATION_NAME} ({EDITION_CODE}).

Compose an appropriate message, which uses any or all of the template’s supported parameters. Each parameter injects variable and dynamic text into a generated message, based on the combination of all relevant factors.

 NOTE:  Each generated message is customized for relevance to the context that triggered it. When any parameter calls upon a database field that is not populated in the necessary context, the generated message shows the parameter label instead of a null value.

{ACCOUNT_IDENTIFIER} — Inserts the same value that the <payload> object returned in its <accountIdentifier> field when your marketplace notified the product vendor about the SUBSCRIPTION_NOTICE event that corresponds to the described change.

{APPLICATION_NAME} — Inserts the full name of the relevant product, exactly as its vendor specified it during marketplace integration.

{BASE_URL} — Inserts an HTTPS link to the fully qualified, DNS-resolvable server hostname that loads your marketplace.

{BILLING_PHONE} — Inserts the telephone number of the billed party or, on a telco-managed marketplace with consolidated billing, inserts the telephone number whose upcoming invoice should itemize any charges for the described transaction.

{COMPANY_COUNTRY} — Inserts the billed company’s country of record on your marketplace.

{COMPANY_PHONE} — Inserts the billed company’s telephone number of record on your marketplace.

{COMPANY_UUID} — Inserts the unique (on your marketplace) identifier for the billed company.

{COMPANY_WEBSITE} — Inserts the billed company’s navigable web address.

{EDITION_CODE} — Inserts the vendor-supplied alphanumeric string — such as RECURRING or PLATINUM or TURBO — that identifies the cited edition outside your marketplace. The vendor populated this parameter while configuring the described edition.

{EVENT_TYPE} — Inserts the API notification label that corresponds to this API event.

{ORDER_CONFIGURATION} — Some vendors modify the purchase flow for their product (at Developer > Products > {product} > [Edit] > Integration | Edit Integration) by checking the Additional product settings required during checkout checkbox and then enforcing your collection of non-standard details from a buyer during purchase. For example, the vendor might request the buyer’s SSH key or prompt the buyer to name a virtual machine. Supposing that the vendor who should receive this message has configured the described product to collect non-standard details during purchase, your use of this parameter inserts the verbatim values that the buyer entered in the non-standard fields during purchase.

{ORDER_ITEMS} — Inserts an inventory of all items on the order.

{PARTNER_LABEL} — Inserts the name of the organization whose channel administrators operate and manage your marketplace.

{PARTNER_STORE_LABEL} — Inserts your marketplace brand name, such as Acme Marketplace.

{SALUTATION} — Inserts a formal and possibly gendered greeting, depending on which native language is the default language on your marketplace. For example, the salutation in English is ‘Dear.’

{SUPPORT_EMAIL} — Inserts the email address that receives technical support requests at your organization. You populate this parameter when you enter a Support Email value in the Support Resources area at Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | General Settings.

{SUPPORT_PHONE} — Inserts the telephone number that receives technical support calls at your organization. You populate this parameter when you enter a Support Phone value in the Support Resources area at Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | General Settings.

{SUPPORT_URL} — Inserts the navigable web address where your organization hosts a knowledge base, peer-to-peer support forum, paid customer support agents, or similar resources for your marketplace. You populate this parameter when you enter a Contact Support URL value in the Support Resources area at Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | General Settings.

{USER_CITY} — Inserts the cited user’s city, town, or village name.

{USER_COUNTRY} — Inserts the cited user’s country name.

{USER_EMAIL} — Inserts the same email address that is the message recipient’s username on your marketplace.

{USER_FIRST_NAME} — Inserts the message recipient’s given name.

{USER_LANGUAGE} — Inserts a citation of the cited user’s preferred language on your marketplace.

{USER_LAST_NAME} — Inserts the message recipient’s surname.

{USER_POSTAL_CODE} — Inserts the cited user’s:

postal code (many countries)

postcode (many countries)

zip code (US and Philippines)

Eircode (Ireland)

PIN code (India)

CAP (Italy)

CEP (Brazil)

or equivalent.

{USER_STATE} — Inserts the cited user’s state, province, or territory name.

{USER_STREET} — Inserts the cited user’s street address or, in some cases, P.O. box.

{USER_TITLE} — Inserts a gendered honorific, when it is both available and known, such as ‘Mr.’ or ‘Ms.’ in English or ‘Herr’ or ‘Frau’ in German.

{USER_UUID} — Inserts the unique (on your marketplace) identifier for the cited user account.

 

Then, use the provided toolbar to format your message body. As needed, you can edit its underlying source by clicking  (Edit HTML Source). You can even insert graphics — such as a corporate logo — by clicking  (Insert/Edit Image).

 

(Optional) Click Send a Sample Email to send yourself a copy of the generated message with all of its parameters populated.

 TIP:  The Subject field and message body field must each contain at least one character, or the Send a Sample Email button does not have any effect when clicked.

Click Save to save your work.

Stop. You have completed this procedure.
