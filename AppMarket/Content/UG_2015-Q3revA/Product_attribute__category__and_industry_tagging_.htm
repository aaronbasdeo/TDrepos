Product attribute, category, and industry tagging basics

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Products

Certain tags that you apply to a product in your catalog determine which listing pages include it. Tags also help consumers to search and filter the catalog in ways that surface a relevant product faster than simple browsing does.

Attribute tags collect products together according to traits that the products themselves share in common, such as their:

type — as in “AppDirect Service” products, for example, or “Domain Provider” products.

supported operating systems — generally Linux, Microsoft Windows, Android, or Apple iOS.

promotional strategy — in bundles or carousels, maybe, or discounted.

Category tags collect products together according to their general purpose, such as office productivity, marketing, analytics, or IT management.

Industry tags collect products together according to the industries where their use might be of particular interest, such as aerospace, hospitality management, or financial services.

Consumers can employ such tags in combination when they browse and shop in your marketplace.

So, imagine that you sell  a configured bundle of IT management products for aerospace workers, and that you have prepared and applied tags for all three of these contingencies.

A consumer can apply such tags as filters and see information about products that match them in combination — with or without any prior knowledge that such products exist or that your marketplace lists them.
