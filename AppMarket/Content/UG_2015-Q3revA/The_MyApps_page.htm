The MyApps page

After you register your account and log in to it, one of the things you can observe is that you now have a MyApps page. Yours shows a personalized tile grid of all the products that you are authorized to use immediately, including free trials and paid contracts.

Your MyApps page is:

A launchpad for your subscribed (and free trial) products.

A control panel to configure certain product-specific behaviors, including who (besides you) can access a given product.

A shortcut to the purchase flows where you can update or upgrade particular orders.

Tile states

The product tiles on your MyApps page might be in any of several general states.

Pending — Indicates an order that has not finished provisioning or de-provisioning, or which stalled during provisioning. Your access to the product is blocked at least temporarily. Orders can stall while the response to an essential request is being processed. For example, you might have to wait while a vendor — Microsoft or Google, let’s say — provisions the product for you or processes your cancellation request. Alternatively, your marketplace might enforce an approvals regimen on all requests that create or update an order. In this case, administrators at your company must confirm that your request is legitimate.

 

Active — Indicates an order that is in good standing for a product that you can use immediately.

Expiring — Indicates an order that is slated to expire in the near future.
