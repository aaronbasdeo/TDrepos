Set sitewide default user role assignments

Before you begin

Log in to your marketplace as its administrator.

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Settings > Settings | Marketplace Functionality

Procedure

Go to Manage > Channel > Settings.

Click past Settings | Marketplace Functionality.

Scroll to the Customer Roles area.

(Optional) Check one or both of the checkboxes next to the User’s Default Role label.

 NOTE:  When neither checkbox is checked, the default role assignment is that of an ordinary enduser.

Set the default role for all new users to be a developer — A developer builds software products, owns the associated copyrights, patents, and trademarks (or works for a vendor that owns these things), and sells access to them through one or more marketplaces. Every developer is associated with a company in the vendor class, so this user role should not be assigned to all users by default unless your company (the provider of your marketplace) sells PaaS and/or IaaS services to a large community of developers. Registered developers can build and edit products, request to integrate these products into your marketplace, and accept or decline requests from the providers of other marketplaces on your same deployment instance to offer for sale the products that he or she has integrated natively to your marketplace. A user with this role has authenticated access to his or her company's own products and API integration events. Developers populate their own product profiles, subject to your oversight. In some cases, a developer can also see and work with information about his or her own bills, customers, and payouts.

Set the default role for all new users to be a company admin — This is typically the person from a given company who first establishes its overall relationship with your marketplace. In addition to standard end-user permissions, he or she can invite the company's staff to join your marketplace as registered users. Company administrators can buy products and assign them to their company’s end-users, and can set rules that govern what their company’s end-users do. Company administrators can access and change all of their company's data. They also have limited authority to manage user permissions.

Click Save Settings.

Stop. You have completed this procedure.
