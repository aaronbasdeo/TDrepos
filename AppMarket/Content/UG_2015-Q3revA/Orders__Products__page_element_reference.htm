Orders (Products) page element reference

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Billing | Orders > [Products]

UI fields and elements on the Products page

Element

Description

Products/Editions (toggle)

Toggles the representation of orders to center on products generally or on product editions specifically.

Show Filters (button)

Exposes a banner above the table, where UI elements can help you to shape which records the table should represent — both in its visible rows and, optionally, in any CSV file that you generate and download.

 

Status (list) — One of the following. Or Show All, the full combination of all the following.

 

Initialized — A purchase order starts life in the INITIALIZED state when it specifies a hosted product whose vendor must provision its accounts AND the order should take effect immediately (so it is not post-dated) AND the order does not apply any discount. Alternatively, the replacement purchase order for a billable and auto-renewable product edition can begin in the INITIALIZED state — immediately upon contract term renewal — either when a channel administrator has blocked in-term contract changes for all products, sitewide OR when the product vendor has blocked in-term contract changes for the specified product in particular.

 

Failed — A purchase order transitions to the FAILED state when the vendor is not able to provision the specified product.

 TIP:  We recommend that you investigate the basis for any failure and work with the vendor to resolve it — both to benefit the affected consumer and so that no similar failures affect your other consumers in the future.

 

Free Trial — A purchase order transitions to the FREE TRIAL state when its vendor finishes provisioning the consumer’s access to its specified free trial edition.

 

Free Trial Expired — A purchase order transitions to the FREE TRIAL EXPIRED state when the associated product’s free trial period has elapsed without its consumer ever upgrading to one of the same product’s billable editions. However, this occurs only in the absence of a defined grace period, which otherwise triggers the FREE TRIAL CANCELED state.

 

Active — The purchase order for a recurrently billable product transitions to the ACTIVE state when its vendor finishes provisioning the consumer’s access to it, and persists in this state until any subsequent transition to a SUSPENDED, CANCELED, or FINISHED state.

 

Suspended — A purchase order transitions to the SUSPENDED state when one of its associated invoices is not paid — and, in the case of a product edition that allows a grace period for late payments, remains unpaid all through its grace period.

 

Canceled — A purchase order transitions to the CANCELED state manually when the consumer cancels the associated subscription OR automatically when a contract that does not renew automatically nonetheless stipulates a grace period during which the consumer is permitted to renew manually — and then the consumer does not renew it.

 

Cost (list) — One of the following.

 

Show All — The full combination of all ordered products.

Paid — Billable products exclusively.

Free — Non-billable products exclusively.

 

Created between (date picker) — Define the start and end of a date range by entering dates manually in the paired date-entry fields, using the notation syntax that is standard in your locale and which your marketplace is configured to use — either MDYY or DMYY. Alternatively, click a field’s corresponding calendar icon () to expose the date-picker widget for populating that field.

 

Show Pending (checkbox) — Causes the table to include pending orders when checked, or to exclude pending orders when cleared.

Download CSV (button)

Generates and downloads to your client system a CSV file whose records are the underlying basis for the Products table as you filtered or otherwise shaped it.

ID (column)

A unique, numeric identifier (often seven digits long) that designates the described purchase order.

Created (column)

The date when the described order was created, using notation syntax that is standard in your locale and which your marketplace is configured to use — either MDYY or DMYY.

Status (column)

Specifies the current state of the described order. See [%=UG_2015-Q3revA.Chapter Title Name%].

Company (column)

Identifies the company whose product contract is tied to the described invoice.

Product (column)

Identifies the contracted product by name.

Frequency (column)

Cites the billing frequency, if any, that applies to the described purchase order’s associated product contract.

Total Fee (column)

A running total and forecast of the payable amount that is scheduled to come due on the next upcoming invoice.

(pagination controls)

Click these UI elements under the table to jump between respectively distant ranges of records in it, when it contains more records than it is configured to render at one time as visible rows.
