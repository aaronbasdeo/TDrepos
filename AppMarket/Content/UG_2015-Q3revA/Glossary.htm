A

activation

A manual process to verify that the email address submitted during user account registration is legitimate. Most marketplaces enforce activation but some do not. When activation is enabled, your marketplace sends a unique link through email to the new registrant’s submitted email address. The registered account becomes activated and available for use only after someone clicks the custom activation link.

add-on

Depending on your marketplace configuration, either our v1 or v2 implementation of supplemental integrated services.

(addons v1) An optional, single-user service or capacity that is sold separately to supplement one product exclusively. Each addon carries its own pricing plan — separate from the associated product — which is often but not always unit-based. Addons might include, for example, a preset amount of additional storage space or time-limited access to expert technical support. You cannot purchase addons for any product that you have not also purchased. Addons are ordered separately and billed separately from the products that they enhance. However:

Some marketplaces do not support the sale or use of addons.

Even on marketplaces where addons are sold, not all vendors sell them.

Some vendors prepare addons for some of their available products but not others.

(addons v2) See Learn about addon versions v1 and v2

API

application programming interface — An intermediary service and framework, which:

specifies the grammar and vocabulary to exchange mutually intelligible instructions and data between:

modular components of a single computer system.

components of multiple, dissimilar computer systems.

a software automation engine and the computer system whose operations it automates.

specifies the hierarchy of entities that can be the subject or object of a communication.

supports reading, and sometimes writing to, values for certain of an entity’s attributes and parameters.

Third-party developers use inbound APIs to integrate their products into AppDirect-powered marketplaces. And marketplace providers sometimes use inbound APIs to build novel UIs for a given marketplace implementation.

AppDirect sometimes uses outbound APIs to integrate marketplaces with external systems — for billing, taxation, customer relationship management, or what have you — or the complex systems that globally prominent SaaS vendors, such as Microsoft, Google, and Symantec, use to host and provision their services.

See also vendor, webhook.

assignment

The process through which a registered user receives access to consume one of the seats that a product contract allocates for consumption.

association

There is more than one kind of “association” on an AppDirect-powered marketplace.

Much of what happens on a marketplace is driven by the fixed relationship between a registered user and the company that sponsors his or her involvement with the marketplace. In fact, a user account cannot exist at all unless it has at least one associated company — although any user might be associated with more than one company. When a user is associated with multiple companies, he or she can choose which company context (meaning the possibly differing user role assignments and product assignments from each associated company) should govern his or her marketplace activities from one moment to the next and can toggle between them easily.

In addition, each reseller is associated in one sense with his or her own company, but also in another sense (“linked”) with every company whose subscribed products and sponsored users he or she supports.

bundle

Varies, according to the context.

lowercase b bundle — A small, defined collection of mutually complimentary products, promoted and sold together collectively, as one unit — typically at a discount, as compared to purchasing them all separately. Consumers can buy the bundled collection of products all at once, as quickly and easily as buying a single product. In aggregate, the products that are joined together in a bundle serve at least one high-level goal of their target consumer. For example, the constituent products in a bundle could all support certain duties that are common among personnel managers at companies with fewer than 100 employees. Or, a bundle might include separate applications that respectively support the accounting, procurement, HR, and marketing goals of a small enterprise. The constituent products in a given bundle might all share their vendor in common, or might not. A merchandising tool to drive marketplace revenue.

uppercase B Bundle — A deprecated product integration type.

C

cancel

The request to revoke a subscription and lift all consumer obligations that the subscription contract imposes. The stipulations in a given subscription contract define the boundaries for if, when, and how a cancellation request can take effect before the defined end date. Penalties may apply.

company

For simplicity’s sake, we use the word “company” to identify any organization that sponsors registered users on an AppDirect-powered marketplace. Organizational sponsorship lends legitimacy and accountability to each registered user account. In fact, no user account can exist or persist on the AppDirect platform without maintaining at least one “company” association at all times.

The four company classes are channel, vendor, reseller, and consumer. In a typical implementation, only one company per marketplace instance has the channel class — because this class is reserved to the marketplace provider, exclusively. Nonetheless, the other three class types can easily apply to dozens or hundreds of registered companies apiece.

A given company’s broad classification is one of the factors that constrains which user roles are even theoretically available for assignment to its associated users. For example, the developer user role is assignable only to registered users whose associated company has the vendor classification. Nonetheless, a given user might maintain multiple company associations at the same time and, through context-switching, explore differing scopes of authority in each of his or her company contexts.

A given company might not even be a company at all, as such, in the real world. It could be a governmental agency, an educational institution, a charitable organization, a self-employed person, or nearly anything else. Any organization can be a “company,” including a larger organization’s constituent divisions, departments, or satellite offices.

A registered user might be associated with more than one registered company at the same time. In this case, the user can switch easily between his or her associated companies, which naturally — because each user role assignment exists in the context of one company association — affects which of his or her user role assignments are currently in effect.

 NOTE:  A channel administrator might designate a given company as a channel, a reseller, or a vendor. These designations, in turn, extend the set of user roles that are available for assignment to the specified company’s associated users.

However, even though channel administrators are empowered to assign the channel class to a registered company, they are not empowered to assign the channel administrator user role to anyone. Authority to assign the channel administrator user role is reserved to your AppDirect account team and (in the case of a provider with the network plan) users in your network who have the corporate administrator user role.

company administrator

See User roles and their scope

The assigned role of any registered user who is correctly empowered to oversee his or her company’s use of an AppDirect-powered marketplace. This user can assign his or her colleagues to product seats, centralize billing for all of the company’s orders, synchronize user accounts and passwords with an Active Directory server, and more.

consumer

Any company that — or any registered user who — obtains products or consumes product seats.

 TIP:  The consumer class is not assigned explicitly to any company. Instead, it is assigned implicitly to every registered company, immediately upon registration.

D

datacenter

A physical location that houses physical servers and other systems that are typically remote from you, which — in the case of an AppDirect-powered marketplace — power cloud services, including XaaS products that your marketplace sells.

developer

See User roles and their scope

A developer builds software products, owns their associated copyrights, patents, and trademarks (or works for a vendor that owns these things), and sells subscriptions to them through one or more marketplaces. Every developer is associated with a company in the vendor class.

discount

A method by which a product vendor or a marketplace provider can reduce consumer expense while promoting one product especially or encouraging conversions generally. At a high level, there are two discount types.

fixed price discount — A coupon (voucher) that reduces consumer costs by a set amount of whichever national or regional currency your marketplace uses as its default. For example, a marketplace whose default currency is USD might offer a fixed-priced discount of $20 USD.

percentage discount — A coupon (voucher) that reduces consumer costs by a whole number percentage of their charged amount. The redemption value of a percentage discount varies according to the purchase price of whichever product edition you redeem it against. For example, it might be 10 percent or 60 percent.

Some discounts are applied automatically upon purchase, while others enforce manual entry of a discount code during the purchase flow.

Some discount codes are redeemable any number of times, while others enforce a maximum number of redemptions.

Some discount codes are redeemable only a set number of times among all registered users who are all associated with the same company. A discount of this type might support no more than three redemptions per company, for example, becoming void immediately for everyone else at a given company after three of that company’s associated users redeem it, even while the same discount remains valid for use by all other companies that haven’t yet passed the equivalent threshold.

Some discounts are applicable to any product whatsoever, while others are applicable to one product exclusively.

Some discounts apply to one billing period alone, after which the marketplace starts or resumes billing at a non-discounted rate. However, other discounts recur during every billing cycle throughout the life of the paid subscriptions that apply them.

discount code

A unique string of characters that identifies one configured discount generally for purposes of configuration or redemption. Depending on how the discount is configured:

Either your marketplace applies its corresponding code to a given purchase automatically, in a case when the discount is correctly applicable, or the consumer must enter the code manually during purchase.

Its redemption might be subject to any of several possible restrictions per user, per company, or per marketplace.

When buyers must enter a text string manually to apply a discount to their orders, the correct text string is exactly the specified discount code.

downgrade

When neither the product developer nor the marketplace provider explicitly forbids doing so, a product’s consumer can switch his or her product subscription from a relatively more expensive edition to a relatively less expensive edition. We consider this a downgrade because lesser editions are frequently less powerful, supporting fewer user stories than their more expensive peers. Alternatively, when edition ranking is enabled on your marketplace, we also consider it a downgrade when the consumer switches his or her subscription to an edition rank that uses a higher number.

E

edition

An edition, sometimes called a product edition or a pricing plan, is one of the orderable versions of a given product. It might be free, billable, or a timed combination of free and billable. Every product that is integrated with a marketplace must have at least one configured edition.

Many products have multiple configured editions available, which each deliver more or less of a given benefit to a given number of users during a given time period in exchange for a given fee. When you compare two editions of the same product, their points of difference could include the minimum contract duration, the billing cycle recurrence, and the existence and length of various grace periods. Furthermore, one might be a stacked edition while the other is a tiered edition.

end-user

See User roles and their scope

entity

A high-level abstraction of the people, places, and things about which an AppDirect-powered marketplace creates and stores records. Entity types include users, companies, products, orders, subscriptions, and payments. See webhook.

event

Any UI-driven or API-driven trigger for a detection-and-notification mechanism that is built in to the AppDirect platform, which can be triggered when:

A significant database entity is created, changed, or deleted.

Any relationship between two or more such entities is established, changed, or terminated.

A system component or integration meets the conditions of a defined metric.

A system component or integration fails a test (exceeds a threshold or range) for acceptable operation.

Events are the basis for all:

server and system logs

records that reports cite

email and SMS messages that notification templates spawn

outbound API requests

webhook triggers

UI messages

exclusion

Subscription contract stipulations can state that the consumers of a given product or product edition are forbidden to maintain concurrent subscriptions to certain other products or product editions. Access to them is mutually exclusive when such stipulations are in effect, and the consumer is explicitly blocked from buying them.

G

grace period

One of these, depending upon the context.

domain grace period — A sitewide, maximum period in days, which channel administrators define for late payments (unpaid invoices) toward domain registration service. This is the count of how many days can elapse after a missed payment before a domain registration becomes suspended for nonpayment.

early termination grace period — No termination fee applies to a canceled subscription during this grace period, supposing that stipulations of the corresponding subscription contract state that it carries any such grace period. In this case, any cancellation after the early termination grace period has elapsed carries the product’s standard termination fees.

lapsed account grace period — The marketplace retains any preexisting user data during this grace period, as a courtesy to former subscribers whose subscriptions could have lapsed in error. But then, after this grace period ends, a lapsed subscriber account and all of its data are deleted automatically.

late payment grace period — A sitewide, maximum grace period in days, which channel administrators define for late payments (unpaid invoices) for any product type except domain registration service. This is the count of how many days can elapse after a missed payment before a subscription becomes suspended for nonpayment. Ordinarily, no grace period whatsoever is allowed for late payments — so, the software-default value is zero (naught) until a channel administrator explicitly changes it. Although the channel administrator can set a maximum number of days that applies as a baseline throughout the marketplace, individual developers can be stricter, by allowing fewer — or no — late days for the products that they control.

I

IaaS

infrastructure as a service — The service provider owns, operates, and maintains the datacenter equipment that its customers use.

instance

The cloud server configuration (virtual CPUs, RAM, file storage capacity, and so on) that operates across any number of physical servers in any number of physical locations to host one public or private installation of one AppDirect-powered marketplace.

integration

An AppDirect-powered marketplace can distribute only the products with whose developers it can communicate successfully, through a shared language (a controlled vocabulary and rigid syntax) called an API. A product that interoperates seamlessly with a given marketplace is fully integrated with it. API events travel freely between the vendor’s servers and the marketplace.

invitation

See invite.

invite

Anyone who has the company administrator user role can invite any additional number of people to register marketplace user account that should likewise be associated with the company administrator’s host company.

Similarly, resellers and reseller managers are also sometimes authorized to invite outsiders to join your marketplace.

L

link

A user with the reseller role assignment is empowered to see and work with order and account details for his or her linked companies and their associated users. The link is established when the reseller creates a company and its first user or when a company administrator makes the explicit choice to trust the reseller. The link is removed when a company administrator removes it.

M

marketplace

An AppDirect-powered marketplace is a commercial XaaS exchange, an online storefront where consumers acquire products — whether SaaS, PaaS, IaaS, or something else entirely — from vendors, sometimes with intervention by resellers, under the oversight of a marketplace provider.

method

See payment method.

MyApps

The page on an AppDirect-powered marketplace where registered users see, manage, and access the products and services that they are authorized to use. Access to most hosted products and services uses SSO but access to private apps does not. Instead, clicking the MyApps tile for a private app opens a login screen for the virtual server where the private app is installed.

N

network

Corporations and other large entities sometimes parcel out the opportunities and responsibilities for marketplace administration among a group of their subsidiaries, so that each subsidiary runs a marketplace of its own. When this occurs, the corporate parent oversees its network of child marketplaces (sometimes called sub-marketplaces) — maintaining ultimate responsibility and ownership while forgoing the overhead of day-to-day administration. See plan.

O

one-time

A billable product or service whose billing never recurs after the initial payment. The product edition is not subscription based.

order

One of the following.

The shared and explicit agreement among a consumer, a vendor, and a provider to complete a well-described transaction, where:

One or the other of these scenarios causes an order to be created:

The consumer requests to obtain at least one product from the vendor, by way of the provider’s marketplace. And when applicable, the consumer affirms that he or she shall pay the legitimate cost.

A sales support representative intervenes, with the consumer’s full and deliberate consent, to place an order faithfully on the consumer’s behalf.

All involved parties agree to work collectively in ways that meet their intersecting requirements and obligations around all transactions that the order encompasses.

The combination of explicit and implicit agreements shared among a consumer, a reseller, a vendor, and a provider to complete a well-described transaction, where:

The reseller intervenes, with the consumer’s full and deliberate consent, to place an order faithfully on the consumer’s behalf.

The consumer requests to obtain at least one product from the vendor, by way of the provider’s marketplace. And when applicable, the consumer affirms that he or she shall pay the legitimate cost.

All involved parties agree to work collectively in ways that meet their intersecting requirements and obligations around all transactions that the order encompasses. However, the reseller is somewhat less central to this effort than its other parties are.

P

PaaS

platform as a service — A hosted environment in which to develop, build, run, and host software. PaaS frees developers from the complexity of building and maintaining their own datacenter.

payment

In a general sense, the receipt of consumer funds by a marketplace provider or a reseller in exchange for a vendor’s billable product.

More specifically, a financial transaction in which a consumer directs funds toward resolving a debt and abiding by a contractual obligation. The currency, payment methods, taxation, and so on, for a given payment are always ones that your marketplace is configured to support and apply.

See also payout and reconciliation.

payment instrument

The combination of one billable party’s payment method and all corresponding details about his or her account with the payment method provider. Details include:

billing information (name, address, telephone number, and so on)

company association

account expiration date

CVV [Is this for North America exclusively?]

payment method

The means, such as a credit card or a digital wallet, by which a consumer pays for a billable product. Supported payment methods often differ between marketplaces and across geographic regions.

payout

Because transactional revenue from a marketplace is split among multiple parties, sometimes one involved party must deduct a sum from its initial receipts to pay another involved party — such as when AppDirect pays a marketplace provider or a vendor. The disbursal of such funds is called a payout. We distinguish between payouts to providers and payouts to vendors.

Channel (provider) payout states are as follows.

INITIALIZED — The described payout has been created for a set of reconciliation lines, but the lines have not yet been associated to the payout. The association is achieved by setting the channel_payout_id of each reconciliation line.

RECONCILED — Backend services successfully linked the described payout to our database reconciliation records and all changes have been persisted.

REPORTING — Backend services are receiving and saving key details about the described payout

REPORTED — Backend services finished saving key details about the described payout.

Vendor payout states are as follows.

FAILED — The described payout failed. For example, processing could not occur because the vendor’s PayPal account configuration was not valid.

MANUAL — The described payout occurred offline — as with a wire transfer or check (cheque) — and was then recorded manually to keep track of the vendor’s balance

PENDING — The described payout was initiated but has not finished processing. This is a transient state because initialization and processing start together in the same one transaction.

SUCCESSFUL — The described payout was processed successfully.

See also payment and reconciliation.

pricing plan

More commonly called an edition or a product edition. See edition.

product

A product is one named item of merchandise that an AppDirect-powered marketplace makes available to consumers through one or more product editions. Such merchandise is often business SaaS but might alternatively be IaaS, PaaS, or from another category altogether.

product catalog

The provider-curated collection of vetted products that a marketplace makes available to its registered users.

product profile

Developers who sell products through an AppDirect-powered marketplace can prepare a separate marketing profile for each of their products. This product profile can include almost any level of detail and is a consumer’s main source of authoritative pre-sales information about the product.

product support representative

See User roles and their scope

provider

The organizational entity (the “company”) that operates an AppDirect-powered marketplace. For example, the provider for your preferred marketplace might be a conglomerate that also operates telecommunication or broadband networks, diversified media, brick-and-mortar storefronts, or a range of financial services.

provisioning

Work that a product vendor or marketplace provider completes to reserve and configure service capacity and related resources for a product seat’s rightful consumer.

purchase

The method through which a consumer obtains access to a product through an AppDirect-powered marketplace, whether the product is billable or not, understanding that “purchase” as such does not connote ownership of anything more than one’s personal data and a contract.

purchase flow

The multi-phase process to purchase access to a billable product for a defined time period or obtain temporary access to a free---trial product.

R

reconciliation

Because all transactional revenue from a marketplace is split among multiple parties, sometimes the marketplace provider must deduct a sum from its initial receipts to pay other involved parties — such as the developer or AppDirect. The calculation of any outstanding payout occurs through a process called reconciliation.

See also payment.

reseller

See User roles and their scope

The assignable reseller user role grants independent merchants sufficient authority to help populate an AppDirect-powered marketplace with consumers (companies and users), and then facilitate any transactions that involve the consumer accounts they created, as well as the transactions of any other companies whose company administrators link to them manually.

reseller administrator

Someone who works on behalf of the marketplace provider to manage and oversee all reseller user accounts, reseller associations, and reseller actions. See link.

S

SaaS

software as a service — A delivery model that supplies access to hosted software (including some amount of remote computational capacity to run it and some amount of remote storage capacity to hold its data and output) upon demand, typically by way of a thin client such as a web browser or a smartphone app.

sales support representative

See User roles and their scope

seat

A consumable unit that reserves one registered user’s right to use a given product for a set length of time. Every product contract stipulates how many seats the corresponding order reserves. A seat is reserved through purchase but consumed through assignment.

SSO

single sign-on — and sometimes single sign-off — A method by which users of one system are authenticated to it automatically and transparently through one of its peer systems. For example, when a marketplace provider has its own dedicated Documentation Center, your marketplace instance is what authenticates your identity (or rejects you as unauthorized) to access technical documentation through SSO. See MyApps and tile.

stacked edition

A stacked edition supports simultaneous ownership of multiple, separate but identical contracts by one consumer, where each instance is also priced identically. A purchased product that shows the Buy More option was purchased in the form of a stacked edition. See also edition and tier.

stipulation

The terms and conditions that a subscription contract imposes are also called its stipulations. The vendor for a given product is likely to impose some of the stipulations in its contract, while AppDirect or the marketplace provider imposes others. Common stipulations include the minimum contract duration, any grace periods, the ability to update, the ability to upgrade or downgrade, exclusion, fees and penalties, the billing frequency, and so on.

subscription

The agreement that binds a consumer, a provider, and a vendor, which grants the consumer access to a paid, freemium, or trial service under the stipulations of a contract to which they are all parties. See one-time.

subscription state

A reserved word or phrase (either ACTIVE, CANCELED, EXPIRED, FAILED, FREE TRIAL, FREE TRIAL EXPIRED, INITIALIZED, or SUSPENDED) that encapsulates the current universe of a given subscription’s provisioning, term, revenue model, uptime, seat consumption, and payment obligations. The lifecycle of a defined subscription carries it through many possible scenarios, back and forth across the range of states. (See [%=UG_2015-Q3revA.Chapter Title Name%].)

tester

tier

One of these:

tiered edition — A product edition that supports volume discounts through the purchase of additive and separately allocatable units — such as gigabytes or users — where the per-unit price drops as the unit count goes up. A purchased product that shows Manage, Upgrade, and Downgrade options on its MyApps page product tile was purchased in the form of a tiered edition. Compare to stacked edition.

multi-tier agreement — A marketplace provider’s business arrangement with a given vendor is “direct” for products in the native catalog, but is “multi-tier” for products in the network catalog. AppDirect sets all product prices in the network catalog, while product developers set the prices for their respective products in the native catalog. [Will have to update this definition when markup pricing is turned on.]

See also plan.

tile

A button on your MyApps page, which is bound to one of your assigned product seats. Clicking a product tile employs SSO to log you in to your account with the corresponding product’s vendor, granting near-immediate access to the product’s features and your data.

U

unit

In the context of a product contract, an allocation that constrains how much of a finite, measurable, and supplemental resource the assigned user can consume before incurring overage costs. A product contract can define and allocate multiple unit types in combination.

For example, a user who is assigned to the “premium” edition of a given product might be permitted to consume 2 hours (units) of premium telephone support per billing cycle and to send 100,000 email marketing messages (units) per billing cycle. Exceeding either threshold adds one or more line items — which might carry a higher than standard per-unit charge — to the corresponding invoice, and might also incur a penalty. See update.

update

When a product has multiple configured editions, its consumers can sometimes switch their respective subscriptions from one number of units to another. The consumer who adds or subtracts units from an active subscription has updated it.

The only exception to this definition is private apps, for which an update either changes the infrastructure or changes the infrastructure provider.

upgrade

When a product has multiple configured editions, its consumers can sometimes switch their respective subscriptions from a lesser edition to a greater one. Lesser and greater in this case are often relative to an edition’s capacity, feature scope, or price. We call this kind of switch an upgrade.

user account

A user account is a set of records that identify one person uniquely on an AppDirect-powered marketplace, including all of his or her activities, settings, orders, payment methods, payments, seat assignments, company associations, reseller links, and so on.

user group

A user group is a logical collection of user records that, collectively, mark the members of a cohort, so that company administrators can assign products and perform other group actions collectively, rather than doing so for one user at a time.

user role

A user role is an assignable range of authority granted to an authenticated user to see and use certain features and services on a marketplace, relative to one at a time of the targeted user’s associated companies. The pool of all user roles that are available for potential assignment to a given user on a given marketplace depends on a combination of:

the associated company’s implicitly received (consumer) or explicitly granted (channel; vendor; reseller) class.

which functions and extended functions the provider’s marketplace is configured to expose.

which AppDirect plan and modules the provider purchased.

Assignable user roles include company administrator , consumer , developer , reseller , reseller administrator , See User roles and their scope.

V

vendor

A vendor is a registered company in the vendor class, which sells a given product or multiple products through a marketplace.

Any user who has the developer role must maintain an active association with at least one vendor. His or her access to developer features is limited to this company context.

verification

A company’s email domain is considered “verified” after a channel administrator determines that it is verifiably unique to that company, and then flags it as such. Then, whenever anyone registers a new user account with an email address in the verified domain, the marketplace associates the correct company to the user account automatically.

W

webhook

A method for one system, such as an AppDirect-powered marketplace, to encrypt and share narrowly defined kinds of information automatically with one or more external systems. These other systems are configured to listen for trusted connections, and then decrypt and process the information upon receipt. Webhook transmissions are event-driven and always specific to a particular entity type.

While APIs wait for and respond to client requests before transmitting a given dataset, webhooks transmit data to their clients in something close to real time, bypassing the requirement for client requests. Specified client systems receive refreshed data on their configured endpoint URLs as soon as events on the host system create, change, or delete records of a given type.

Providers can configure an AppDirect-powered marketplace to:

Share information with its webhook client systems following the creation, modification, or deletion of any:

user account

registered company

user-and-company association

reseller registration

product subscription

sales lead

sales opportunity

quote

payment instrument

Authenticate itself to its webhook client systems via OAuth 2, via tokens, or not at all.

See also API.
