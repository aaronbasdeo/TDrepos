Work with order details

You can explore the fine-grained details of any order, in isolation from all other orders.

Navigation path

Manage > Channel > Marketplace > Billing | Orders > {order}

Before you begin

Log in to your marketplace as its administrator.

Procedure

Go to Manage > Channel > Marketplace.

Click past Billing | Orders.

Click an Orders table row whose underlying record you want to review.

A modal popup opens, which cites the corresponding order’s:

metadata — including the order number, the order creation date, and the order’s current state (as in, Pending, Active, Suspended, or Canceled)

billing and contract terms — including the price per line item, the quantity and utilization of contracted seats, the billing frequency, the billing end date, the next upcoming invoice date, the applied tax rate, and the product terms and conditions

product and edition — including names of the ordered product and edition, as well as details of all the product’s configured and available pricing plans

history — of order state transitions

activity — tracking the initial purchase, any contract modifications, any access requests, seat assignments, and seat revocations that our UI calls unassignments. [There is no such word as “unassign” in the English language.]

consumers — including which registered user placed the order (or placed the order on another user’s behalf), which registered users are assigned to consume the contract’s available seats, which user roles are assigned to the users who consume these seats, and which registered company is bound by the contract’s terms

component products — when the described order pertains to a bundle, rather than a standalone product

In addition, the modal includes links and buttons that help you to:

contact the ordered product’s consumer through email

edit, update, or cancel a subscription

add a reseller association to the order, or edit its reseller association

apply a discount code to the order

(Optional) Do any or all of the following.

Do you want to see an order’s metadata?

Check one order’s metadata

To see:

The order number — Look under the title bar.

The order’s current state — Look under the title bar, next to the action button.

The order creation date — Look in the third column, under the Created label.

Do you want to see the described order’s billing and contract terms?

Check basic aspects of one order’s billing and contract terms

To see:

The price of any line item — Click the order number. Scroll to the Items area in the child modal. When done with the child modal, click the title bar [X] icon to close it.

 

How many seats the order reserved — Look for the Usage label and indicator in the second column.

 

How many seats the product’s assigned users have consumed — Look for the Usage label and indicator in the second column.

 

The billing frequency — Look for the Frequency label, above the Activity tab.

 

The billing end date — Look for the Billing Ends label under the action button.

 

The next upcoming invoice date — Look for the Next Invoice Date label under the action button.

 

The applied tax rate — Click the order number. Scroll to the Items area in the child modal. When done with the child modal, click the title bar [X] icon to close it.

 

The product terms and conditions — Click the order number. Scroll to the bottom of the child modal and click the terms and conditions link. When done with the child modal, click the title bar [X] icon to close it.

Do you want to learn which product and edition were ordered?

Check the names of an ordered product and edition

To see:

The name of the ordered product — Look for the product name in the title bar.

The name of the ordered product edition — Look for the edition name in the second column, under the Edition label.

Do you want to see all of the ordered product’s configured editions?

Learn about all available pricing plans for the ordered product

Click the Update Subscription tab.

A product can have any number of configured editions. Thus, it might be necessary to scroll through the list before you can see all of them.

Do you want to see the described order’s history of order state transitions?

Check the order’s history of state transitions

Click the Order History tab.

Do you want to see the described order’s activity history?

Check the activities log for one order

To see:

The original purchase date — Look in the third column, under the Created label.

The dates of any contract modifications — Look for any “{username} modified the subscription” entries on the Activity tab.

The dates of any access requests — Look for any “{username} is requesting access” entries on the Activity tab.

The dates and recipients of any seat assignments — Look for any “{username} assigned {product} - {edition} to {username}” entries on the Activity tab.

The dates of any seat revocations — Look for any “{username} unassigned {product} - {edition} from {username}” entries on the Activity tab.

Click the title bar [X] icon to close the modal.

Stop. You have completed this procedure.
