The sequence of transitions in Scenario 18

The chronology of transitions in this scenario is as follows.

Order and subscription state transitions in Scenario 18

 

A newly placed order starts its life in the PENDING USER APPROVAL state when a reseller (or other authorized representative of your marketplace) has placed an order on behalf of a registered user.

There is no subscription state at this stage. A subscription does not yet exist.

 

Transition to a PENDING REMOTE CREATION state occurs after the registered user consents to this assisted purchase — and after your marketplace notifies the product vendor that an order was placed, which requires provisioning (here, the requested edition is billable) — and when both of these statements are true simultaneously:

The order specifies a hosted product whose vendor must provision it.

The order should take effect immediately, and therefore is not post-dated.

The subscription state is INITIALIZED.

 

Transition to an ACTIVE state occurs when the relevant vendor finishes provisioning. The consumer is now truly subscribed to a billable product that carries a recurring billing cycle.

The subscription state is ACTIVE.

 

Finally, the transition to a CANCELED state occurs (manually in this case) when the consumer requests to terminate the subscription that a given order covers (rather than merely updating the subscribed edition or units). However, stipulations of the corresponding subscription contract might:

Mandate that the subscription must continue, uninterrupted, through a minimum stipulated number of billing cycles.

Impose penalties for early cancellation.

Therefore, cancellation itself might take effect after a future billing cycle concludes.

The subscription state is CANCELED.
