Table sorting patterns

Many tables support re-sorting their records by the values in one column or another.

Column heading labels that are blue — such as Customer ID in the first screenshot here or Created in the second one — are clickable, and, clicking them re-sorts the table’s rows accordingly.

Column heading labels that are black — such as Active in the first screenshot here or Frequency in the second one — are not clickable. You cannot re-sort the table by the value under a black column heading.

Where the values in a column are numeric, they sort in numeric (or reverse-numeric) order.

Where the values in a column are letters, they sort in alphabetical (or reverse-alphabetical) order.

Where the values in a column combine numbers with letters, they sort in alphabetical order, but numerals sort before the letter A.

 TIP:  Click a column heading twice to toggle its imposed sorting order. For example, when a table is sorted alphabetically by the A–Z values in one of its columns, click that column’s heading to flip its sorting order to Z–A.
